# Fiskaler IRNR Evaluation: 65% on External Benchmark

## Context

On 2026-03-20, we ran fiskaler — our Spanish fiscal knower — against 20 official exam questions on the Impuesto sobre la Renta de No Residentes (IRNR). The questions come from Editorial Tecnos, a major Spanish legal publisher, accompanying university-level tax law textbooks.

This was the first external benchmark evaluation for any of our knowers. Previously, fiskaler scored 10/10 (100%) on our self-authored case study — a complex digital nomad scenario involving Beckham Law eligibility, social security irregularity, and Modelo 720 thresholds.

## Result

**Score: 13/20 (65%)**

| Metric | Value |

## What She Got Right (13/20)

Fiskaler correctly answered questions on:
- IRNR territorial principle (Q1)
- Diplomatic immunity and IRNR subjection (Q3)
- EP regime — worldwide income attribution (Q4)
- Donations to non-residents — ISD not IRNR (Q5)
- IRNR vs IS distinction for non-EP income (Q6)
- Transfer pricing between EP and head office (Q7)
- Non-resident attribution entities as taxpayers (Q8)
- Complementary levy not applicable with CDI (Q11)
- Athlete prize money subject to IRNR (Q13)
- EU interest exemption (Q15)
- Royalties with CDI limits (Q16)
- Withholdings dispense declaration duty (Q17)
- Optional IRPF regime for EU residents — average rate method (Q19)

## What She Got Wrong (7/20)

### CDI Interaction Errors (3 questions)

**Q2: CDI function.** Answered A ("establish methods of taxation for residence/source state"). Correct: C ("contain allocation rules excluding source state taxation"). Fiskaler understood CDIs broadly but missed the precise legal characterization — CDIs allocate, they don't create taxing powers.

**Q12: Dividends to non-residents.** Answered A ("subject to IRNR regardless of CDI"). Correct: C ("not subject when CDI contains exclusive allocation rule"). Fiskaler defaulted to "always subject" when the correct answer depends on the specific CDI article — some allocate dividends exclusively to the residence state.

**Q14: Brussels lawyer working in Spain.** Answered C ("obtained in Spain, CDI doesn't exclude, but exempt"). Correct: B ("obtained in Spain but not subject per CDI employment income rule"). Confused "exempt" with "not subject" — legally distinct concepts in Spanish tax law.

### EP Regime Errors (3 questions)

**Q9: EP deductible expenses.** Answered C ("interest, technical assistance, own funds deductible"). Correct: B ("management expenses unrelated to activity deductible"). Reversed which expenses are and aren't deductible for EP base calculation.

**Q10: Construction EP base.** Answered B ("transfer pricing regime or 15% on costs"). Correct: A ("income minus expenses"). Confused the simplified method with the general rule.

**Q18: Attribution entities with non-resident members.** Answered B ("entity pays IRNR on attributed income"). Correct: A ("entity doesn't pay IRNR; non-resident members pay via EP regime"). Misunderstood the attribution mechanism — income passes through to members.

### Special Levy Error (1 question)

**Q20: Special levy on non-resident real estate entities.** Answered A ("not levied on rental companies"). Correct: B ("deductible expense in IRNR"). Both statements about the levy are actually true, but the question asked about its tax treatment (deductible), not its exceptions (rental companies).

## Error Pattern

The 7 errors cluster in two areas:

1. **CDI interaction with IRNR (3 errors)** — fiskaler confuses when a CDI *excludes* taxation (not subject) versus *limits* it (reduced rate) versus *exempts* it (subject but exempt). These are legally distinct concepts with different procedural consequences.

2. **EP regime specifics (3 errors)** — detailed rules about what expenses are deductible for EP base calculation, how construction EPs determine their base, and how attribution entities relate to EP taxation.

## Interpretation

Fiskaler reasons excellently through complex real-world scenarios. In the self-authored case, she identified Beckham Law eligibility, flagged the urgent 6-month deadline, calculated both IRPF paths with concrete numbers, and produced a prioritized action plan. All correct.

But on precise technical questions — the kind that appear on government exams — she scores 65%. The gap is not in reasoning ability. It is in technical precision on edge-case rules: CDI allocation mechanics, EP base calculation, attribution entity pass-through.

This is the value of external benchmarks. Self-authored cases test whether the knower can think through a problem. External exams test whether she knows the precise answer to a specific technical question. Both matter. We were only measuring one.

## What This Means

- **65% is a baseline**, not a verdict. It tells us where to focus learning.
- **CDI interaction rules** and **EP regime specifics** are the two weak subdomains.
- **Learning will use a separate question pool** — never the evaluation questions. See our [verification protocol](/knowledge/solarient/consultant-verification-protocol) for the separation methodology.
- **The next evaluation** will include IRPF and IVA questions from Agente de Hacienda Publica official exams, broadening subdomain coverage.
